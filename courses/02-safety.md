Safety is non-negotiable. This course covers the four universal rules of gun safety, range etiquette, and how to store firearms responsibly.

## The four rules of gun safety

These rules are repeated everywhere for a reason: they work. Treat them as absolute. Violating one can get someone killed; stacking violations multiplies risk.

1. **Treat every gun as if it is loaded.** Even if you just cleared it, even if someone handed it to you "unloaded." No exceptions. This mindset prevents the one time the gun actually was loaded from becoming a tragedy.

2. **Never point the muzzle at anything you are not willing to destroy.** The gun is only "pointed" when the muzzle could send a bullet into a person, pet, or thing you value. Keep the muzzle in a safe direction at all times—at the range, at home, during cleaning, during handoffs. "Safe direction" means toward a backstop or the ground, never at people.

3. **Keep your finger off the trigger until your sights are on the target and you have decided to shoot.** Rest your finger along the frame or outside the trigger guard. Guns don't "just go off"; they go off when the trigger is pulled. Discipline with your finger is the last line of defense.

4. **Be sure of your target and what is beyond it.** Bullets can pass through walls, brush, and soft targets. Know where the round will stop. On a range, that's the backstop. In the field or in a defensive situation, you are responsible for every round until it stops.

If you internalize these four rules, you have the foundation for a lifetime of safe handling. Everything else—range rules, storage, transport—builds on them.

## Range etiquette

- **Listen to the range officer.** They set the rules for that facility (cease-fire, hot/cold range, where to point, when to go downrange).
- **Only handle guns when the range is "hot" (live fire) and it's your turn,** or in designated safe areas. Never handle a gun or point it toward the firing line when others are downrange.
- **Respect the firing line.** Don't step past it during a cease-fire. Don't sweep anyone with your muzzle when moving or turning.
- **Ear and eye protection.** Wear both. So does everyone around you.
- **If you see something unsafe, say something.** A polite "muzzle" or "finger" reminder can prevent an accident. Nobody is above the rules.

## Secure storage

How you store guns when not in use depends on your situation: kids in the house, risk of theft, need for quick access in an emergency.

- **Locking options** — Cable locks through the action, trigger locks, or locked cases prevent casual access. Many new firearms ship with a cable or lock. Use them if quick access isn't required.
- **Safes and lockboxes** — A fire-rated safe or a steel lockbox secures guns from unauthorized use and from theft. Biometric or keypad lockboxes can allow fast access for a bedside gun while still keeping it away from others.
- **Ammunition** — Storing ammo separately from the gun adds another layer of safety, especially with children in the home. It does slow down readiness for self-defense, so this is a tradeoff you make for your own context.
- **Legal obligations** — Some states require that firearms be stored locked or unloaded when not in the owner's immediate control. Know your local laws.
